In a dramatic development for German politics, Jens Spahn, a prominent leader of Chancellor Friedrich Merz’s party, has resigned from his post. The sudden decision follows an intense public and internal party debate.

The controversy arose after Spahn announced that he and his husband had welcomed a baby via a surrogate mother in the United States. Surrogacy remains illegal under German law, leading to severe pressure from conservative colleagues.
