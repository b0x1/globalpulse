Iqaluit, the capital of Nunavut and Canada's northernmost city, has officially launched its first-ever public bus line. The service is being managed by Jacinto Marques, who originally emigrated from Angola.

The new bus service represents a major milestone for local transportation in the Arctic community, where extreme winter conditions make commuting a challenge. Residents have welcomed the transit option as a step toward modernization.
